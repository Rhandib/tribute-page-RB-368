Neil deGrasse Tyson

Neil deGrasse Tyson is an American astrophysicist, author, and science communicator known for popularizing science through his books and television appearances, including hosting the series "Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey." He is also the director of the Hayden Planetarium in New York City.

Timeline

1958

Born in New York City, Neil deGrasse Tyson developed an early passion for astronomy after visiting the Hayden Planetarium.

1973

At the age of 14, Neil received a scholarship from the Explorers Club to view a solar eclipse aboard the SS Canberra with notable figures like Neil Armstrong.

1991

Earned a PhD in Astrophysics from Columbia University.

1996

Appointed director of the Hayden Planetarium, leading a major reconstruction to modernize the facility.

2004

Served on the Moon, Mars and Beyond commission and received the NASA Distinguished Public Service Medal.

2006

Hosted NOVA ScienceNow.

2009

Launched the StarTalk radio show.

2014

Hosted Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey, a spiritual successor to Carl Sagan's original series.

2015

Began hosting StarTalk on National Geographic Channel.

2016

Appeared in films like Ice Age: Collision Course, Zoolander 2, and Batman v Superman.

2024

MERLIN'S TOUR OF THE UNIVERSE published. Neil has authored 30 works, including "Astrophysics for People in a Hurry" and "Death by Black Hole."
